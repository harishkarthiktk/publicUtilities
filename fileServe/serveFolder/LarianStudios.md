# Bootstrapped vs Public Companies — Key Takeaways

### Ownership & Governance
- **Bootstrapped / Private (e.g., Larian Studios)**
  - Founder-led, no public shareholders
  - Internal governance, coherent creative vision
- **Public (e.g., Ubisoft)**
  - Shareholders + board oversight
  - Decisions shaped by market expectations

---

### Release Cadence
- **Bootstrapped**
  - One flagship product at a time
  - Long cycles (5–7+ years)
  - Heavy post-launch support
- **Public**
  - Many projects in parallel
  - Frequent, predictable releases
  - Revenue continuity prioritized

---

### Core Trade-Offs

**Time Horizon**
- Bootstrapped: long-term value
- Public: quarterly performance

**Risk**
- Bootstrapped: concentrated, high-upside bets
- Public: diversified, lower volatility

**Capital**
- Bootstrapped: limited → focus & efficiency
- Public: abundant → scale & speed

**Incentives**
- Bootstrapped: quality, reputation, longevity
- Public: revenue timing, forecasts, monetization

**Culture**
- Bootstrapped: craft, ownership, patience
- Public: process, metrics, predictability

**Accountability**
- Bootstrapped: cash flow, team, customers
- Public: shareholders, analysts, market sentiment

---

### When Bootstrapping Works Best
- High differentiation products
- Quality compounds over time
- Community trust matters
- Depth > speed

---

### Bottom Line
Bootstrapping trades **scale and predictability** for **control, patience, and creative upside**.  
Public companies trade **control and flexibility** for **capital, stability, and throughput**.
